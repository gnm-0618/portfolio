Dr. Salim Ali

Birdman of India

Here's a time line of Dr. Salim Ali

  • 1896- born in Bombay, Bombay Presidency, British India
  • 1917-18- Studied and completed a degree in Zoology From St. Xavier's College, married Tehmina Begum.
  • 1926- Secured a position of a guide at the museum of the Bombay Natural History Society. He conducted the visitors and instructed them about the preserved birds.
  • 1929- Visited Germany and visited Dr. Irvin Strassman, a world famous ornithologist.
  • 1930- Lost current job position at the museum and joined the museum as a clerk. Published a research paper discussing the nature and activities of the weaver bird.
  • 1939- Wife died following a minor operation.
  • 1958- The Government of India honored him with a Padma Bhushan.
  • 1959- Received the assistance of Swiss ornithologist Alfred Schifferli in India.
  • In the early 1960s, the national bird of India was under consideration and Salim Ali was intent that it should be the endangered Great Indian bustard, however this proposal was over-ruled in favour of the Indian peacock.
  • 1963- attempted a citizen science project to study house sparrows through Indian birdwatchers subscribed to the Newsletter for Birdwatchers.
  • 1967- Published the book "Common Birds". Became the first non-British citizen to receive the Gold Medal of the British Ornithologists' Union.
  • 1976- The Government of India honored him with Padma Vibushan.
  • 1985- Published his engaging autobiography "The Fall of Sparrow".
  • For his extraordinary efforts, he was given an international award of INR 5 lacs, but he donated all the money to Bombay Natural History Society.
  • 1987- On June 20, this genius man died after a prolonged battle with prostate cancer.
  • He received honorary doctorates from the Aligarh Muslim University (1958), Delhi University (1973) and Andhra University (1978).
